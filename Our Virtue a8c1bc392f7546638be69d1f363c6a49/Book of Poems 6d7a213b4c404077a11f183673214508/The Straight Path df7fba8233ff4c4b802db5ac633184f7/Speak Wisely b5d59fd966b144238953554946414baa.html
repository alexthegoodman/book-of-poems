Speak Wisely

As for the petty sins

Do not condemn them

For you are more alike

With a humble heart and foresight

As for the deeds of others

Do not criticize them

Aim for higher ways, lighter ways

Ask questions, make suggestions

Deception is perverse speech

Lies are false intel for the deciding person

Falsehood is the brother of theft

Greater sin is found in the liar

Our God is a God of truth

Accept this guidance, O People

Do away with intentional lies

Followed by the remaining falsehoods

Corrections are shortcuts

While better to be enacted than ignored

They come from pride and haughtiness

Indeed, corrections shut out new insights

Making excuses for offenses

Leads one blindly into hateful speech

Wise people take them lightly

They are also mindful of people's feelings

Given emergencies and weight of importance (or upon witnessing a heinous sin)

Wise people will cause offense and correct others

For wise people seek not after short-term gains

But the great wealth of knowledge which leads to life

One who desires failure

And to reveal their hidden agenda

This is the one who tries convincing others

Without evidence, science, or knowledge

He pleads and persuades and creates impressions

The convincing people rely not on solid matter

The one who is convinced, later he feels duped

And he himself is given to temptation

What pressuring speech it is, to convince a person

Rather, the wise person will teach of the truth

And his friend will know better, and they will both be successful

Give in to no pressuring speech

It is a friend of force, it is a foe to respect
